About the Project

My Body, My Care

A youth-led initiative transforming how adolescent girls in rural Nigeria understand and manage their menstrual health and intimate hygiene.

  • ✓ Funded by the Global Youth Mobilization Youth Empowerment Fund
  • ✓ Started in Ile-Ife, Osun State — expanding to more communities across Nigeria

  • ✓ Providing hygiene kits and continuing education resources

What We Do

How We Make a Difference

Our project focuses on four key areas to ensure girls receive comprehensive support and education.

📚

Health Education

Age-appropriate, medically accurate education on menstrual health, intimate hygiene, and body changes delivered by healthcare professionals.

🎒

Hygiene Kit Distribution

Providing girls with essential hygiene products and supplies so they can manage their periods safely and with dignity.

👩

Confidence Building

Creating safe spaces where girls can ask questions, share experiences, and build confidence in managing their health.

🏫

School Engagement

Partnering with secondary schools to reduce absenteeism caused by poor menstrual hygiene and lack of knowledge.

Why This Matters

The Challenge We're Addressing

In rural Nigerian communities, menstrual health remains a silent barrier to girls' education and well-being.

📈 School Absenteeism

Many girls miss school during their periods because they lack access to hygiene products and knowledge about menstrual health.

💡 Limited Knowledge

Myths and misconceptions about menstruation create fear, shame, and confusion among adolescent girls.

⚠ Health Risks

Poor intimate hygiene practices increase the risk of preventable infections that affect girls' health and well-being.
