Menstrual Health

Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

📅

What Is Menstruation?

Menstruation (having a period) is a natural process where the lining of the uterus sheds through the vagina. It typically lasts 3-7 days and occurs every 21-35 days. It is a sign that your body is healthy and functioning normally.

⚙

Your Menstrual Cycle

The menstrual cycle is the monthly process your body goes through to prepare for a possible pregnancy. It involves hormones like estrogen and progesterone that control changes in your ovaries and uterus.

📈

When to Expect Your Period

Most girls get their first period between ages 10-16. After puberty begins, it can take 1-2 years for your cycle to become regular. Tracking your cycle can help you know when to expect your next period.

Intimate Hygiene

How to Practice Good Hygiene

💧 During Your Period

  • ✓ Change pads or tampons every 4-6 hours
  • ✓ Wash your intimate area with clean water
  • ✓ Use mild, unscented soap on the outer area only
  • ✓ Always wipe from front to back after using the toilet
  • ✓ Wear clean, dry underwear
  • ✓ Wash your hands before and after changing hygiene products

✨ Daily Hygiene Tips

  • ✓ Bathe or shower daily with clean water
  • ✓ Change your underwear every day
  • ✓ Wear breathable cotton underwear
  • ✓ Avoid using perfumes or scented products on your intimate area
  • ✓ Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet
  • ✓ Change out of wet or sweaty clothes promptly

⚠ When to See a Doctor

  • ● If your period is extremely painful
  • ● If your period suddenly becomes very heavy
  • ● If you notice unusual discharge or odor
  • ● If you experience itching or irritation
  • ● If your period doesn't come for 3+ months (and you're not pregnant)
  • ● Any time something doesn't feel right
Myths vs Facts

Separating Facts from Myths

Many myths surround menstruation. Here are the facts.

❌ MYTH

"You shouldn't bathe during your period."

✓ FACT

Bathing during your period is not only safe, it is important for good hygiene. Regular bathing helps keep your body clean and prevents infections.

❌ MYTH

"You can't exercise during your period."

✓ FACT

Exercise during your period is perfectly safe and can actually help reduce cramps and improve your mood. Light exercise like walking or stretching is beneficial.

❌ MYTH

"Menstruation is dirty or shameful."

✓ FACT

Menstruation is a completely natural and healthy biological process. There is nothing dirty or shameful about it. Millions of girls and women experience it every month.

❌ MYTH

"You can't get pregnant during your period."

✓ FACT

While less likely, pregnancy is possible during your period, especially if you have a shorter cycle. Sperm can survive in the body for several days.
