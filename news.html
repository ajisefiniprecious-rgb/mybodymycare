Milestone July 2026

110 Girls Reached in First Phase

We are proud to announce that we have educated 110 girls and distributed 110 hygiene kits across three secondary schools. This marks a major milestone in our project funded by the Global Youth Mobilization Youth Empowerment Fund, and we're just getting started on our goal of reaching 5,000 girls by 2030.

The sessions covered menstrual health, intimate hygiene practices, and body confidence. Each girl received a hygiene kit containing sanitary pads, soap, wipes, and educational materials in both English and Yoruba.

Healthcare professionals from OAUTHC were present to answer medical questions and provide expert guidance. Schools visited include Community Secondary School, and two others in the Ile-Ife area.

"This is just the beginning," said project lead Ajisefini Precious Boluwatife. "Our vision is to reach 5,000 girls across Nigeria by 2030, and this first phase proves that the model works and the need is real."

Event July 2026

School Visit to Community Secondary School

Our team, alongside healthcare professionals, conducted an interactive menstrual health education session at Community Secondary School. Girls learned about their bodies, asked questions, and received hygiene kits.

The session began with an overview of the menstrual cycle, followed by a practical demonstration on proper hygiene practices. Girls were encouraged to ask questions in a safe, judgment-free environment.

Many of the students shared that they had never received this kind of education before. Some had been using unsafe methods during their periods, while others had missed school out of fear and shame.

By the end of the session, each student received a hygiene kit and a printed educational booklet they could take home and share with their families.

Partnership June 2026

Healthcare Professionals Join Our Mission

We partnered with qualified healthcare professionals to ensure the accuracy and quality of our health education content. Their expertise helps us provide medically sound information to the girls we serve.

Our healthcare partners include nurses and medical practitioners from the Obafemi Awolowo University Teaching Hospitals Complex (OAUTHC). They reviewed all our educational materials and participated in school visits to answer questions directly.

"Having real healthcare professionals in the room changes everything," said one school administrator. "The girls trust them, and the information they receive is accurate and reliable."

This partnership ensures that every piece of advice, every hygiene tip, and every educational resource we provide is backed by medical expertise.

Announcement September 2025

Global Youth Mobilization Grant Awarded

We are excited to announce that "My Body, My Care" has been selected as a recipient of the Global Youth Mobilization Youth Empowerment Fund. This grant enables us to bring menstrual health education to girls in rural Nigeria.

The Youth Empowerment Fund, delivered through the Global Youth Mobilization and funded by the European Union, supports youth-led projects addressing health and well-being challenges in communities around the world.

After a competitive application process, our project was one of the selected initiatives from Nigeria. The grant of EUR 1,500 will fund hygiene kit production, educational materials, school visits, and community outreach sessions.

"This grant is more than funding," said Ajisefini Precious. "It's a vote of confidence in young people who want to make a difference in their communities."

Update June 2026

Hygiene Kits Prepared for Distribution

Our team has assembled 110 hygiene kits containing essential products for menstrual management. Each kit is designed to provide girls with the supplies they need to stay healthy and in school.

Each kit contains sanitary pads, antibacterial soap, wet wipes, a small bag for discreet carry, and an educational pamphlet with hygiene tips in both English and Yoruba.

The kits were assembled by team volunteers and quality-checked to ensure every item met hygiene standards. Distribution was coordinated with school administrators to reach every girl in need.

"For many of these girls, this is the first time they've had access to proper hygiene products," said the project team. "It's a small thing that makes a huge difference."

Community July 2026

Community Leaders Embrace the Project

Before launching in schools, we engaged with community leaders in our founding community of Ile-Ife to gain their support and understanding. Their endorsement has been crucial in making this project a success as we expand to more communities.

Meetings with traditional leaders, parent associations, and school administrators helped us understand the unique needs of each community and address cultural sensitivities around menstrual health education.

"When community leaders support an initiative like this, it opens doors," said the outreach team. "Parents trust them, schools listen to them, and the girls feel safe knowing the community stands behind them."

This community-first approach has been key to the project's acceptance and success, and will continue to guide our expansion to new areas across Nigeria.
