Our Story

How It All Started

My Body, My Care was born from a simple observation: too many adolescent girls in rural communities were missing school, suffering in silence, and lacking basic knowledge about their own bodies.

Data from the Obafemi Awolowo University Teaching Hospitals Complex (OAUTHC) revealed that its gynecology department had the third-highest number of patients the previous year, many adolescent girls suffering from preventable hygiene-related infections. The pattern was clear: most of these issues stemmed from a lack of basic intimate hygiene education.

In many rural communities in Nigeria, Hygiene and menstruation seems like a taboo topic. Girls often receive little or no education about menstrual health, leading to fear, shame, and preventable health issues. Many girls miss several days of school each month simply because they don't have access to hygiene products or the knowledge to use them safely.

Determined to change this, we applied for and won the Global Youth Mobilization Youth Empowerment Fund, giving us the resources to make a real difference. We launched in Ile-Ife, Osun State and we're just getting started. Our goal is to bring intimate hygiene education and resources to thousands more girls across Nigeria.

What We've Done

Our Journey So Far

Since receiving the grant, we have taken concrete steps to make an impact — and this is just the beginning.

1

Received the Global Youth Mobilization Grant

Selected as a recipient of the Youth Empowerment Fund, giving us the resources to turn our vision into reality.

2

Partnered with Healthcare Professionals

Collaborated with qualified healthcare professionals from OAUTHC to develop medically accurate, age-appropriate health education content.

3

Visited Three Secondary Schools

Delivered menstrual health education sessions and distributed hygiene kits at three secondary schools in Ile-Ife — our first community.

4

Educated 100 Girls

Reached 100 adolescent girls with comprehensive education on menstrual health, intimate hygiene, and body confidence.

5

Distributed 100 Hygiene Kits

Every girl who participated received a hygiene kit containing essential products for safe and dignified menstrual management.

Our Goals

What We Aim to Achieve

We started in Ile-Ife. Our vision is to reach every girl who needs us.

🌱

Reach 5,000 Girls by 2030

Scale from 100 girls in our first community to 5,000 girls across multiple states, bringing intimate hygiene education and resources to girls who need it most.

📚

Create Lasting Educational Resources

Develop resources that continue to educate girls even after each school visit, including this website and multilingual educational booklets.

🤝

📈

Reduce School Absenteeism

Measure and demonstrate a reduction in school absenteeism caused by poor menstrual hygiene management in every community we serve.
