Our Story

How It All Started

My Body, My Care was born from a simple observation: too many adolescent girls in rural communities were missing school, suffering in silence, and lacking basic knowledge about their own bodies.

In many rural communities in Ile-Ife, Osun State, Nigeria, menstruation remains a taboo topic. Girls often receive little or no education about menstrual health, leading to fear, shame, and preventable health issues. Many girls miss several days of school each month simply because they don't have access to hygiene products or the knowledge to use them safely.

Determined to change this, we applied for and won the Global Youth Mobilization Youth Empowerment Fund - giving us the resources to make a real difference.

What We've Done

Our Journey So Far

Since receiving the grant, we have taken concrete steps to make an impact.

1

Received the Global Youth Mobilization Grant

We were selected as one of the recipients of the Youth Empowerment Fund, providing us with the resources to implement this project.

2

Partnered with Healthcare Professionals

We collaborated with qualified healthcare professionals to develop accurate, age-appropriate health education content for adolescent girls.

3

Visited Three Secondary Schools

Our team visited three secondary schools in rural Ile-Ife to deliver menstrual health education sessions and distribute hygiene kits.

4

Educated 100 Girls

We have reached 100 adolescent girls with comprehensive education on menstrual health, intimate hygiene, and body confidence.

5

Distributed 100 Hygiene Kits

Every girl who participated in our program received a hygiene kit containing essential products for safe and dignified menstrual management.
